# Biosynthesis of Silver Nanoparticles from Selected Cyanobacteria and their Antibacterial Potential

This repository documents a wet-lab research conducted as part of M.Sc. thesis work in Phycology at Visva-Bharati University. The study explores the green synthesis of silver nanoparticles (AgNPs) from eight cyanobacterial strains sourced from the Visva Bharati Culture Collection of Algae (VBCCA), and evaluates their antibacterial activity against six multidrug-resistant (MDR) bacterial pathogens using a resazurin-based microtiter plate assay.

---

## Key Technical Implementations

- **Cyanobacterial culture and identification —** Strains were cultured in BG-11 medium (with and without nitrogen) under controlled conditions (25±1°C, 7.5 W/M², 12h light/dark cycle) and identified morphologically using Desikachary's *Cyanophyta* monograph under a Leica DM 750 trinocular microscope.
- **Green synthesis of AgNPs from aqueous cyanobacterial extracts —** Biomass from eight strains (*Anabaena* sp. 31-N, *Anabaena* sp. 46-N, *Anabaena* sp. SUB-4 (-N), *Nostoc* sp. 222-N, *Oscillatoria* sp., *Mastigocladus* sp. 74-N, *Lyngbya aestuarii*, and *Lyngbya majuscula*) was powdered, extracted in distilled water, and mixed with AgNO₃ solution (1mg/2ml) at 30°C for 48 hours. This biological route was chosen over chemical and physical synthesis methods for being cost-effective, low-maintenance, and environmentally non-toxic.
- **UV-Visible spectroscopy for AgNP confirmation —** Biosynthesis was confirmed by the characteristic Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR) peak in the 320–580 nm range, using a Shimadzu UV-1800 240V spectrophotometer. SPR peak position reflects nanoparticle size and monodispersity, and all positive syntheses fell within the expected window (429–460 nm).
- **Resazurin microtiter plate assay for antibacterial screening —** A 96-well plate assay was used to screen all eight AgNP preparations against six MDR bacterial strains at a working concentration of 100 µg/ml. The resazurin indicator turns pink upon reduction by metabolically active bacteria; wells that remained blue confirmed growth inhibition. Controls included: Ciprofloxacin as positive control, and DMSO solvent & only bacterial suspension as negative control.

---

## Key Biological Insights

- AgNPs were successfully synthesized from six of the eight strains, as confirmed by SPR peaks in the 429–460 nm range. *Anabaena* sp. 46-N and *Oscillatoria* sp. did not produce a clear SPR peak, suggesting either insufficient reduction activity or extract composition differences that did not support nanoparticle nucleation under these conditions.
- *Lyngbya aestuarii* produced the highest absorbance peak (1.322 at 460 nm), indicating the most robust synthesis, while *Anabaena* sp. SUB-4 (-N) yielded the lowest (0.297 at 429.50 nm).
- Seven of the eight AgNP preparations showed broad-spectrum antibacterial activity against all six MDR test strains — both Gram-positive (*B. cereus*, *B. subtilis*, *L. monocytogenes*, *S. aureus*) and Gram-negative (*S. typhi*, *E. coli*). The exception was *Anabaena* sp. 31-N, which showed activity only against *B. cereus* and *S. aureus*, suggesting a narrower inhibitory profile possibly linked to nanoparticle size or surface chemistry.
- The results support the potential of cyanobacterial AgNPs as alternative antibacterial agents, particularly relevant in the context of rising antibiotic resistance.

---

## Notes and Future Directions

This repository serves primarily as a record of the experimental work underlying the thesis. The study establishes proof-of-concept for phyco-synthesized AgNPs as MDR antibacterials, but stops short of full physicochemical characterization. Future work could include FTIR (to identify biomolecules capping the nanoparticles), SEM (morphology and size distribution), and XRD (crystallinity), which together would complete the characterization pipeline. Extending the antibacterial assays to MIC determination and testing anticancer activity — which has been reported for AgNPs from related cyanobacterial genera — would be the natural next steps.
